Speaker 1: Modifying the Document Structures Given the array of capabilities described above, it is very simple also to provide for very flexible manipulation of the file structure. For operating on a small, basic set of structure entity nouns, essentially the same basic verbs may be used as for text manipulation, that is Insert, deeleet, moove, Copy, Replace, and Transpose are quite sufficient for most cases. For instance, "moove Branch 2 b to follow 3 c" immediately moves Statement 2 b and all of its substatements to follow Statement 3 c, and their statement numbers are automatically changed from 2 b, 2 b 1, et cetera., to 3 d, 3 d 1, et cetera.
Speaker 1: A few extra verbs are useful for structure manipulation. For instance, a Break command will break a given statement off at a designated point in its text string, and establish the rest of the text as a new, separate statement. And an Append command does the reverse, that is, it appends the text of one or more existing statements to the end of a designated statement.
